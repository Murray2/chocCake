Murray's
Molten Chocolate Cake Tarts

This is a recipe I have modified slightly from the Commander's Palace in New Orleans. Five awesome ingredients in one dish, so simple and delicious. This recipe comes from the same restaurant that brings us our school's jalapeno cornbread. It is just the right amount for about 4 large chocolate cake portions (or 6 smaller ones). If you want to scale this recipe up, these cakes freeze and microwave nicely. As long as you don't reheat them too long, the middle will be a runny chocolate sauce.

Recipe:

  • 6 tbsp of butter, softened
  • 1/2 lbs semi sweet chocolate
  • 4 eggs
  • 3/4 cups of sugar
  • 3/4 cups of flour

Procedure:

Preheat your oven to 350 degrees fahrenheit

Use 2 tablespoons of the butter to grease 4 ramekins or oven proof coffee cups, silicon, or muffin tins. Check to see that all surfaces are completely covered with butter especially the corners.

Place the remaining 10 tablespoons of butter with the chocolate (in small pieces) in the top part of a double broiler or in a medium bowl set over a saucepan of barley simmering water. Stir until it is a smooth butter ganache, DO NOT OVER COOK for your chocolate will become grainy.

Break the eggs into the work-bowl of the food processor (or bowl with a wisk). Add the sugar and beat until air is incorporated. Using theÂ creaming method, proceed until the mixture is smooth and fluffy.

Pour in chocolate and butter ganache mixture: mix for 10 seconds.

Sift the flour in and process for another 10 seconds. Scrape down the inside of the work-bowl with a spatula, and process until smooth, about another 10 seconds more.

Pour an equal amount of the batter into each ramekin so they cook at the same speed. Place the chocolate cakes in the oven and bake in a preheated oven.

Pulling these out of the oven at the right time is the key. The mixture begins to rise in the center and is still runny, if it rises too much they are over cooked (but they still taste like delicious chocolate cakes). Remove the souffles from the oven, and let them cool in the fridge. If they are over cooked you can just eat them right away.

Once they have cooled down you can remove them from the baking container and plastic wrap them.


Once cooled down the middle is hard so you can put them in the refrigerator and microwave them for 25 seconds when you want one. Or you can freeze them if you have a lot and want to save some for another time. You can microwave them from frozen.

*If you want extra molten sauce in the middle, you can freeze small balls of the batter and place it in the middle of the cake batter before you bake them. ENJOY!
